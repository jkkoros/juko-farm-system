Coffee Pests & Diseases

Early detection and effective control for healthy trees

Common threats in Kenyan Arabica plantations

Coffee Berry Disease (CBD)

Symptoms
  • Dark sunken lesions on green berries
  • Berries turn black and mummify
  • Orange-pink spores on infected berries
  • Premature berry drop
Control Measures
Cultural: Prune for good aeration, remove mummified berries
Chemical: Copper-based fungicides (e.g., Nordox, Kocide) at flowering
Timing: Spray at early flowering and repeat every 3â€“4 weeks during wet seasons
Resistant varieties: Batian, Ruiru 11

Coffee Leaf Rust (CLR)

Symptoms
  • Yellow-orange powdery spots on underside of leaves
  • Leaves turn yellow and fall prematurely
  • Severe defoliation reduces yield
Control Measures
Cultural: Proper spacing, pruning for light penetration
Chemical: Triazole fungicides (e.g., Tebuconazole, Hexaconazole)
Timing: Apply at onset of long rains and repeat as needed
Resistant varieties: Batian, Ruiru 11 (highly resistant)

Coffee Berry Borer

Symptoms
  • Small hole at the tip of cherry
  • Tunnels inside berry with larvae
  • Damaged beans (low quality)
Control Measures
Cultural: Timely & complete harvesting, strip picking
Sanitation: Remove all fallen and leftover berries
Chemical: Insecticides only if infestation >5% (consult extension officer)
Traps: Brocap or alcohol-based traps

Antestia Bug

Symptoms
  • Black spots on cherries
  • "Potato taste" defect in cup
  • Deformed or aborted berries
Control Measures
Cultural: Encourage natural enemies (birds, wasps)
Monitoring: Scout weekly during flowering & fruit development
Chemical: Pyrethroids if population high
Shade management: Avoid excessive shade
